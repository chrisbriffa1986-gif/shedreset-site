## RATIONALE

**The Board reached rare, near-unanimous convergence — I am ratifying the consensus, not forcing it.**

- **The aperture conflict (Conflict 1) is resolved: Car-first, not car-only.** Vega opened with car-only on clarity grounds; Sol and Muse both revised *toward* him, conceding that car interiors are the sharpest acquisition wedge — clearest demo, strongest value context, and direct continuity into the agreed valeter lane. But all three landed on the same nuance: **lead with car, retain furniture.** I am adopting the majority's "car-led / car-first" over Vega's stricter "car-only" because it captures Ledger's reach argument without a positioning tax — the furniture use case is sold in the two-pack, not the ad. This is the one decision that genuinely needed a vote, and the seats effectively pre-voted it.

- **Ledger made the financial case decisive, not merely corroborating.** The CFO revised toward the car frame on margin, not taste: the furniture frame drags the anchor to the £8–20 pet-hair shelf and runs **underwater on first-order CAC** (£5.36 contribution against £12–25 estimated cold CAC), while the car frame clears £14.25 contribution and survives a £14 CAC. His correction — anchor the CAC gate to *contribution margin, not retail price* — is adopted. His two-pack ruling ("keep the headline car-only; let the bundle sell the sofa") is the mechanism that reconciles reach with margin. All pricing figures are MEDIUM confidence, COGS-assumed.

- **The emotional register (Conflict 2) is settled on pride/readiness.** Vega opened with embarrassment ("apologising when a passenger gets in"); Muse rejected shame explicitly on reputation grounds — a brand that repeatedly implies dog owners are dirty becomes unlikeable. Sol and Vega both revised toward practical urgency. I am ruling with Muse and Sol: **"Your dog had the back seat. Reset it for passengers."** This satisfies our non-negotiable reputation constraint — we do not buy thumb-stops by insulting the customer.

- **The claims discipline is unanimous and binding.** No "60-second," "fast," "one swipe," "embedded hair," or "better than a vacuum" ships unless a continuous, unstaged demonstration substantiates it. No fake passenger disgust, staged shame, invented scarcity, or fabricated valeter endorsements. This is a hard line, not a preference.

- **The four kills stand, agreed across all seats:** grooming/deshedding tool (KILL), lint-roller replacement (KILL — commodity shelf), eco/reusable (DEMOTE to proof point), novelty/"oddly satisfying gadget" (KILL — novelty is not preference).

- **B2B is real but unproven.** Valeters/detailers first, manually validated before trade infrastructure is built. Ledger confirms the car frame is the *only* aperture where wholesale is financially real (£13–15 trade price leaves both parties margin; the furniture frame collapses the lane). Confidence remains LOW — no buyer has said yes yet.
