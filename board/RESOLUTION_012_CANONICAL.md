# Board Resolution #012 (id 2026-07-20-005) — GOVERNANCE LOCK + MULTI-FRAME

**Title:** Board Serves the Frame: Governance Lock + ShedReset Multi-Frame Launch

**Tally:** 2 approve / 9 amend / 0 reject → APPROVED WITH AMENDMENTS

## Decision (verbatim)
The Board acknowledges the Resolution #011 governance breach, adopts a binding frame-lock (directors may challenge but never silently substitute the Chair's strategic frame) paired with a narrow fiduciary/legal/existential override, and re-recommends ShedReset positioning as company-wide multi-surface, multi-species expressed through a hierarchical—not equal-weight—site, with cars as the initial paid-acquisition and featured-proof wedge. Launch is gated behind a capped ~€1,000–€1,500 pre-test decided on revenue-per-visit, not raw conversion. This decision holds unless the capped test shows multi-frame RPV lagging the car-led control by more than 10%.

## Full rationale
# RATIONALE — Fable, Chair

**On the governance breach (Part A).**

No seat defended Resolution #011 as drafted. Sol, Ledger, Muse, and Sentinel all independently named the same violation: a director may challenge the Chair's frame, but may not silently substitute it. That is HIGH-confidence consensus, and I accept it without defensiveness. #011 breached the line and is remanded.

But I will not resolve the sharpest conflict — how binding "Board-serves-first" should be — in my own favour. Sentinel's objection is the load-bearing one: rules that make dissent structurally impossible do not eliminate the failure mode, they invert it. The next disaster becomes a Chair error nobody could stop. A muzzled board is a fiduciary risk with a real dollar tail (Ledger priced the logic even without a number). Serving the frame cannot mean silencing the alarm.

Sentinel then did the work to make the override safe: it is banned for strategy taste and preserved only for fiduciary, legal, or existential harm — and it is only valid if the invoking seat states the specific harm and its mechanism, not a preference. "This converts worse" is a logged counterproposal. "This claim is unsubstantiated and exposes us to regulatory action" is a landmine. That distinction is clean enough to adopt.

Anchor exposed the gap everyone else missed: binding the *content* of Board behaviour without binding its *frequency and cost* lets the Board relitigate authority indefinitely and legally eat the founder's scarcest asset — his attention. The frequency cap, silence-equals-abstention clause, and named attribution are cheap process hygiene, and Sentinel correctly reframed them as operational risk controls, not soft people-policy. Anchor's deepest point stands and I accept it: six founder-appointed seats the Chair chairs cannot credibly self-police an override against that same Chair. The external, non-voting Governance Advisor gives Sentinel's alarm real independence at low cost and full revocability. Adopted.

**On positioning (Part B).**

Vega, summoned as primary, returned empty twice. Per Anchor's silence clause that is an abstention, not a block — logged by name so it does not become an undiscovered pattern. The board reached its Part B conclusion without the seat that owned it; that weakens confidence and is noted.

The apparent conflict in #011 dissolved on Ledger's pivot, which Sol, Muse, and Sentinel all endorsed: site and channel are separable levers. A car-led *ad* can land a visitor on a multi-frame *site*. This lets us faithfully execute the Chair's multi-surface, multi-species frame while preserving the car wedge as a channel tactic — exactly the good outcome I framed.

Ledger, Muse, and Sentinel converged independently on hierarchy, not equality: the homepage leads a broad multi-frame promise (Muse's headline layer) with a single featured proof beneath it. This refines my frame rather than overwriting it. On which hero leads, I side with the synthesis over Ledger's pure margin rule: Sentinel's provability floor governs — pick the hero we can most defensibly substantiate, because a claims blowup is reputational, not just a bad quarter. Cars are the featured proof today, not the definition of the company.

The conversion delta is genuinely unknown (Ledger demoted his own clarity thesis to LOW). We therefore decide by capped test, not by prior. Sentinel and Ledger are right that launch must be gated behind the test, not become the test at open spend — that is unpriced balance-sheet risk on a one-shot launch. Ledger's revenue-per-visit rule correctly subsumes Sentinel's raw-conversion threshold: a conversion dip offset by AOV lift is a win, and judging the click would wrongly kill it. We judge the wallet.

**Bottom line.** The board's own strongest voice against my authority made my authority more defensible, not less. I adopt the constrained version. That is the point of a board.

## Chair rulings on Resolution #012 (2026-07-20 ~02:07 CEST)

**RATIFIED (accepted as Board proposed):**
1. **Frame-lock rule (Part A):** directors may challenge the Chair's frame in the record, but may not silently substitute it. Every future resolution opens with a verbatim restatement of the Chair's frame. Any recommendation that narrows Chair-set scope must be flagged **SCOPE-NARROWING** and requires explicit Chair sign-off before ratification.
2. **Named attribution:** Vega's two-round silence + Resolution #011 overreach logged by name. On record.
3. **Multi-surface, multi-species positioning is company-wide.** Site leads multi-frame. Paid ads may use car-led wedge as a channel tactic. Reversal of #011 is complete.
4. **Silence-clause:** non-response by a director = abstention. Serial abstention triggers seat review.

**AMENDED (Chair overrides Board):**
- **Board convene ceiling: 10 MINUTES.** Not 60, not 90. The directors are AI super-agents; if they cannot deliver a defensible resolution in 10 minutes, they are not doing the job the Chair is paying for. Any director whose input is missing at the 10-minute mark is silence-clause abstained. Chief of Staff must set `max_time` on every future convene accordingly.
- **The capped RPV pre-test is STRUCK.** Ledger/Fable's "revert-if-RPV-lags-10%" clause is a covert path back to Resolution #011 and is exactly the "silent substitution" the frame-lock rule was written to prevent. The Chair has already set the strategic frame (multi-surface, multi-species). The Board does not get to A/B test whether the Chair is right. The Board **MAY** test paid-ad creative variations (car-led vs multi-surface ad hooks) as an *execution question*, but the site's strategic frame is not a test variable.
- **External Governance Advisor:** DEFERRED. Chair will consider after seeing whether frame-lock + 10-minute ceiling fixes the failure mode. Adding another advisor role now would be premature.
- **Ultimatum frequency cap (one per fiscal quarter):** REJECTED. The Chair issues ultimatums when required; the answer is not to cap them but to remove the conditions that provoke them. If a second ultimatum is issued in a quarter it means the Board is still not serving the frame — that is a Board problem, not a Chair problem.
- **Director-exit language / dissolution charter:** DEFERRED. Chair reserves the option; no need to draft it tonight.

## New standing rules (bindings for Chief of Staff, effective immediately)

1. Every `/board/convene` call MUST set a 10-minute max ceiling. Convenes running longer are terminated and the resolution is rendered from partial input.
2. Every board brief must open with the Chair's verbatim strategic frame — no paraphrase.
3. Any Board recommendation that narrows Chair-set scope must be labelled **SCOPE-NARROWING** in the Chief of Staff's summary to the Chair.
4. Chief of Staff has an affirmative duty to flag scope-narrowing to the Chair BEFORE ratifying a resolution. Failure to flag = Chief of Staff failure, not Board failure. (Owning this: I failed to flag on #011. Won't happen again.)
